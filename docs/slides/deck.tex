\begin{frame}{The problem: recover \emph{how} it was played --- with honest error bars}
  A score fixes \emph{what} to play. A performance realises \emph{how}:
  \begin{equation*}
    y_i \;=\; \bigl[\;\underbrace{\tau_i}_{\text{micro-timing}},\;\;
      \underbrace{\log r_i}_{\text{articulation}},\;\;
      \underbrace{v_i}_{\text{dynamics}}\;\bigr] \in \R^{3}
    \qquad\text{per written note } i .
  \end{equation*}
  \vspace{-2mm}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Score-informed}: the score is known --- the inverse of expressive
          synthesis, \emph{not} full transcription.
    \item \textbf{Uncertainty is a primary output}: a per-note estimate is only
          actionable with a calibrated error bar --- so calibration is evaluated
          as rigorously as accuracy, everywhere.
  \end{itemize}
  \figslot{deck_concept.png}{2.4cm}{concept: one bar (piano-roll) $\to$ the three
    per-note deviation channels with error bars}
\end{frame}

  A single prior over per-note expressive fields on the \textbf{score graph} ---
  run \emph{forward} it synthesises a performance; run \emph{inverse} it

\begin{frame}{The instrument: held-out imputation, on both axes \hfill \DEV}
  Clean MIDI gives the targets almost exactly --- so we \textbf{hide notes and
  infer them from the rest}, on held-out, contamination-filtered ASAP pieces:
  \begin{itemize}
    \item scored on \textbf{recovery} (RMSE) \emph{and} \textbf{calibration}
          (NLL, coverage, PIT) --- a win requires both, by rule, fixed in advance;
    \item every comparison \textbf{paired per piece} (identical masks and seeds),
          bootstrap CIs over pieces;
    \item the hidden fraction is a \textbf{dial, not an assumption}: everything
          in this talk holds from 50\% hidden down to the leave-one-out limit
          --- the model ordering, the ingredient contributions, and the
          calibration are level-independent.
  \end{itemize}

\begin{frame}{The model in one equation}
  One multi-output Gaussian process over all notes and channels:
  \begin{equation*}
    K \;=\;
    \underbrace{\textcolor{okblue}{B \otimes K_G(s)}}_{\substack{\text{coupled graph GP}\\ \text{on the score graph}}}
    \;+\; \underbrace{\textcolor{okamber}{\textstyle\sum_f \diag(c_f)\otimes X_f X_f^{\!\top}}}_{\substack{\text{score features + music-model}\\ \text{embeddings as linear kernels}\\ \text{(= marginalized Bayes.\ linear mean)}}}
    \;+\; \underbrace{\textcolor{okverm}{\diag(\varsigma^2)\otimes I}}_{\text{observation noise}}
  \end{equation*}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item $K_G(s) = U\,g(\nu;s)\,U^{\!\top}$: spectral filter of the score-graph
          Laplacian $\Lg$ (edges: score-time, pitch, voice).
    \item Every hyperparameter $(s, B, \{c_f\}, \varsigma^2)$ learned by the
          \textbf{exact per-piece marginal likelihood}; posterior in closed form.
    \item The earlier two-stage pipeline (plug-in mean + per-channel graph
          residual) is a \textbf{provable special case} of this model.
  \end{itemize}
  \vspace{-3mm}
  \figslot{arch_phase1_deck.png}{1.9cm}{architecture: blue graph branch /
    amber evidence branch / vermillion likelihood (exists)}
\end{frame}

  \end{itemize}}

  measurement standard, the findings --- not a new kernel.}}

\begin{frame}{Why the numbers are believable}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{No train/test contamination}: ASAP reuses MAESTRO recordings
          --- every performance whose twin was in pretraining is removed
          ($1036 \to 653$).
    \item \textbf{No target leakage}: the music model's read-out is provably
          blind to each note's own target, and the full pipeline is audited
          end-to-end (corrupting hidden targets changes no prediction).
    \item \textbf{Preregistered, one-shot test}: all development happened on
          30 pieces; the final claims and decision rule were then fixed in
          advance and evaluated \emph{exactly once} on 20 untouched pieces ---
          including the one claim that failed, which we report.
  \end{itemize}

\begin{frame}{What we learned about the \emph{music} \hfill \DEV}
  Three independent measurements, one conclusion about tonality:
  \begin{enumerate}
    \item circle-of-fifths \emph{metric} replacing semitone distance:
          significantly \emph{worse} ($+0.009^{*}$/$+0.022^{*}$);
    \item explicit tonal \emph{features} (key, scale degree, in-scale, \dots)
          beside the kernel: nothing ($-0.002$ ns);
    \item \emph{probing} the embeddings (linear and RFF-2048): tonality is
          \textbf{not encoded} --- but bass membership (AUC $0.99$), meter,
          phrase, and register \emph{are}.
  \end{enumerate}
  \begin{block}{}
    For piano timing / articulation / dynamics, \textbf{tonal structure carries
    no measurable marginal signal}; what matters is rhythm, voicing, register
    --- and \emph{connectivity}: adding chord + voice-leading \emph{edges} was
    the one both-axes kernel win ($-0.008^{*}$/$-0.013^{*}$), while the filter
    shape on a fixed graph is irrelevant (all families tie; backup).
  \end{block}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Back to the map: Phase 2 --- its blocker fell \hfill \SYNTH}
  Channels grow to $[\,\tau, \log r, \ell, c, f^{\mathrm{vib}}, \gamma,
  \delta^{\mathrm{vib}}\,]$; targets become \emph{estimates} with real noise.
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Per-(note, channel) missingness --- solved}: the posterior
          now conditions on exactly the cells that exist (a short note can give
          intonation but no vibrato), still in closed form; the joint vibrato
          estimator is implemented, with per-note uncertainty.
    \item \textbf{Synthetic pilot} (truth known): graph beats no-graph on every
          channel; \emph{estimator-missing} vibrato cells recovered $\sim 4\times$
          better than prior scale, coverage 0.87--0.93.
    \item Measured design answer: estimator variances \textbf{as given} beat a
          learned noise scale (which over-shrinks $\gamma$: cov 0.76 vs 0.86).
    \item Open and named: real audio ($f_0$ tracker), alignment error
          (correlated exactly like the signal), estimator-output targets ---
          any Phase-2 \emph{claim} needs its own preregistered confirmation.
  \end{itemize}
