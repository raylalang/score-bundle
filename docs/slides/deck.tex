\begin{frame}{Task: score-informed performance transcription}
  The score is known. We infer how each written note was played:
  \begin{equation*}
    y_i \;=\; \bigl[\;\underbrace{\tau_i}_{\text{micro-timing}},\;\;
      \underbrace{\log r_i}_{\text{articulation}},\;\;
      \underbrace{v_i}_{\text{dynamics}}\;\bigr] \in \R^{3}
    \qquad\text{per note } i .
  \end{equation*}
  \vspace{-2mm}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item This is the inverse of expressive synthesis, not full transcription.
    \item The output is a per-note posterior: a point estimate and a calibrated
          error bar. Calibration is evaluated as rigorously as accuracy.
  \end{itemize}
  \figslot{deck_concept.png}{2.4cm}{concept: one bar (piano-roll) and the three
    per-note deviation channels with error bars}
\end{frame}

% ================================================================ 3
\begin{frame}{Research program: one model, three kinds of observation}
  A single GP prior over per-note expressive fields on the \textbf{score
  graph}. Run forward it synthesises a performance; run inverse it transcribes
  one. Across phases, \textbf{only the likelihood changes}:
  \begin{center}
  {\footnotesize
  \begin{tabular}{@{}llll@{}}
  \toprule
  Phase & Observation & Channels & Status \\
  \midrule
  0 \; music model & MIDI corpora (MAESTRO) & --- & done; feature provider \\
  1 \; piano & performance MIDI, per note & $\tau, \log r, v$ & \textbf{done, confirmed} \\
  2 \; mono.\ pitched & $f_0$-derived targets, noisy & $+\,c$, vibrato & machinery built, pilot passed \\
  3 \; waveform & the audio itself & all latent & scoped; conjugate parts tested \\
  \bottomrule
  \end{tabular}}
  \end{center}
  This talk: the Phase-1 result, then the current state of Phases 2 and 3.

\begin{frame}{Evaluation: held-out imputation, scored on both axes \hfill \DEV}
  Clean MIDI gives the targets almost exactly, so the task is constructed:
  hide a fraction of notes, predict them from the rest.
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Data: held-out, contamination-filtered ASAP piano pieces.
    \item Metrics: RMSE for recovery; NLL, coverage, and PIT for calibration.
          A win requires improvement on both, by a rule fixed in advance.
    \item All comparisons are paired per piece (identical masks and seeds),
          with bootstrap confidence intervals over pieces.
    \item The hidden fraction is not an assumption. Every conclusion in this
          talk holds from 50\% hidden down to the leave-one-out limit.
  \end{itemize}
  \vspace{-1mm}
  \figslot{masksweep_dev.png}{2.9cm}{RMSE vs hidden fraction (50\% to LOO,
    three systems) and paired ingredient CIs per level (exists)}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{The model in one equation}
  One multi-output Gaussian process over all notes and channels:
  \begin{equation*}
    K \;=\;
    \underbrace{\textcolor{okblue}{B \otimes K_G(s)}}_{\substack{\text{coupled graph GP}\\ \text{on the score graph}}}
    \;+\; \underbrace{\textcolor{okamber}{\textstyle\sum_f \diag(c_f)\otimes X_f X_f^{\!\top}}}_{\substack{\text{score features + music-model}\\ \text{embeddings as linear kernels}\\ \text{(= marginalized Bayes.\ linear mean)}}}
    \;+\; \underbrace{\textcolor{okverm}{\diag(\varsigma^2)\otimes I}}_{\text{observation noise}}
  \end{equation*}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item $K_G(s) = U\,g(\nu;s)\,U^{\!\top}$ is a spectral kernel of the
          score-graph Laplacian $\Lg$ (edges: score-time, pitch, voice).
    \item All hyperparameters $(s, B, \{c_f\}, \varsigma^2)$ are fit by the
          exact per-piece marginal likelihood. The posterior is closed-form.
    \item The earlier two-stage pipeline (plug-in mean, then a per-channel
          graph residual) is a special case of this model.
  \end{itemize}
  \vspace{-3mm}
  \figslot{arch_phase1_deck.png}{1.9cm}{architecture: blue graph branch,
    amber evidence branch, vermillion likelihood (exists)}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Evaluation hygiene}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{No train/test contamination.} ASAP reuses MAESTRO recordings;
          every performance whose twin was in pretraining is removed
          ($1036 \to 653$).
    \item \textbf{No target leakage.} The music model's read-out is provably
          blind to each note's own target. The full pipeline is audited
          end-to-end: corrupting hidden targets changes no prediction.
    \item \textbf{Preregistered one-shot test.} All development used 30 pieces.
          The final claims and decision rule were fixed in advance, then
          evaluated exactly once on 20 untouched pieces. The one claim that
          failed is reported.
  \end{itemize}
  \vspace{3mm}
  \centering

\begin{frame}{Replication on 30 unseen pieces \hfill \REPL}
  The full ablation was re-run on 30 additional pieces that were never used

\begin{frame}{Findings: what predicts expressive deviations \hfill \DEV}
  Three independent measurements agree about tonality:
  \begin{enumerate}
    \item Replacing semitone distance with a circle-of-fifths metric makes
          results significantly worse ($+0.009^{*}$/$+0.022^{*}$).
    \item Adding explicit tonal features (key, scale degree, in-scale flags)
          changes nothing ($-0.002$ ns).
    \item Probes of the embeddings (linear and RFF-2048) find no tonal
          information. They do find bass membership (AUC $0.99$), meter,
          phrase, and register.
  \end{enumerate}
  \begin{block}{}
    Tonal structure carries no measurable signal for piano timing,
    articulation, or dynamics. Rhythm, voicing, and register do.
    Adding chord and voice-leading edges helps ($-0.008^{*}$/$-0.013^{*}$);
    changing the spectral filter on a fixed graph does not (backup slide).
  \end{block}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Phase 2 status: intonation and vibrato \hfill \SYNTH}
  Channels grow to $[\,\tau, \log r, \ell, c, f^{\mathrm{vib}}, \gamma,
  \delta^{\mathrm{vib}}\,]$. Targets become estimates with real noise.
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Per-(note, channel) missingness is solved: the posterior conditions
          on exactly the cells that exist (a short note can give intonation
          but no vibrato), still in closed form. The joint vibrato estimator
          is implemented, with per-note uncertainty.
    \item Synthetic pilot with known ground truth: the graph beats the
          no-graph ablation on every channel. Vibrato parameters the estimator
          could not identify are recovered about $4\times$ better than prior
          scale, with coverage 0.87 to 0.93.
    \item Measured design choice: use estimator variances as given. A learned
          noise scale over-shrinks $\gamma$ (coverage 0.76 vs 0.86).
    \item Still open: real audio, $f_0$ tracking, alignment error. Any Phase-2
          claim needs its own preregistered confirmation.
  \end{itemize}
  \vspace{-2mm}
  \figslot{arch_phase2_deck.png}{1.6cm}{Phase-2 architecture: only the
    likelihood block changes (exists)}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Backup: the Gaussian tail and the Student-$t$ fix \hfill \DEV}
  \begin{itemize}
    \item Failure mode (one confirmation piece and one dev replica): a very
          steady piece gives a tiny fitted timing scale; a few outlier notes
          then explode the pooled NLL while RMSE and coverage stay normal
          (dev replica: NLL $+34.7$, coverage 0.90).
    \item Student-$t$ timing likelihood (EM prototype, unit-tested): on the dev
          tail cell, NLL goes from $+34.7$ to $\bm{-0.96}$ under its own
          predictive. RMSE and coverage are unchanged. The EM weights isolate
          the outlier notes.
    \item On tail-free pieces it costs a small premium.
    \item It is applied to no thesis number: the confirmation set is spent, so
          the fix waits for its own preregistered confirmation.
  \end{itemize}
\end{frame}
